Principles & Guidelines of Good Website Design

To effectively apply these principles, it is important to understand how users interact with websites and their behavioral patterns.

Understanding User Behavior: Users' habits on the web are similar to customers' habits in a store. They quickly scan pages, click on links that catch their interest, and ignore large portions of the page. Users look for something interesting and clickable, and if a page doesn't meet their expectations, they click the back button and continue searching. Users value quality content over design.

Web Design Guidelines:

  • Users scan rather than read. They look for anchors to guide them through the page's content.
  • Users expect instant gratification and will leave a website if it doesn't meet their expectations or has a complex navigation.
  • Users don't always make optimal choices. They satisfice by choosing the first reasonable option.
  • Sequential reading flow doesn't work on the web. Users follow their intuition and muddle through the information.
  • Users want control over their browsing experience and prefer consistent data presentation.
  • The web writing style should be adjusted to users' preferences, using concise phrases, scannable layouts, and objective language.
  • Keep the design simple and focus on providing the necessary information.
  • Effective use of white space helps reduce cognitive load and enhances information perception.
  • Use a visible language that organizes information, economizes visual elements, and communicates effectively.
  • Conventions in design improve usability by reducing the learning curve and meeting users' expectations.
  • Test the design early and frequently to identify and address problems and improve the user experience.
