The Impact of the Peak-end Rule on User Experience

The peak-end rule influences our perception of events by focusing on the most significant moments and endings. Our memory tends to simplify experiences and judge them based on the intensity of peak moments and the final moments.



In a study, participants preferred a longer but more gradual aversive experience over a shorter and less intense one. This suggests that duration plays a minor role in our recollection of aversive experiences.

The peak-end rule is influenced by memory bias and recency bias. Emotionally intense events, or peaks, leave a stronger impression on our memory. Additionally, we tend to remember the beginning and end of an experience more vividly due to the recency bias.


To enhance user experience, consider the following:

  1. Identify the peak moments in the customer journey. Journey mapping helps uncover both frustrating and delightful moments, allowing you to address pain points and capitalize on positive experiences.
  2. Create memorable and positive peaks. Improve the low points by implementing solutions that enhance the user's experience. Amplify the high points by replicating similar positive experiences throughout the journey. Add elements that bring convenience, comfort, and delight to make the experience memorable.
  3. End on a positive note. The final moments hold significant weight in evaluating an experience. Counteract negative occurrences by providing a positive ending, such as enjoyable music, a free sample, or a sense of accomplishment.
